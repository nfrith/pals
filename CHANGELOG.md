### ALS-074
- Compatibility: migration_required, refresh_required
- Summary: First real `als_version: 1 → 2` hop. Reclassifies the Delamain dispatcher as a first-class construct on both sides of the boundary. The plugin canonical relocates from `nfrith-repos/als/skills/new/references/dispatcher/` to root-level `nfrith-repos/als/delamain-dispatcher/` (sibling to `statusline/` and `delamain-dashboard/`). Each operator system gets one installed construct copy per deployed Delamain at `<system_root>/.als/constructs/delamain-dispatcher/<delamain>/`, seeded from the canonical's vendor-declared `construct.json.source_paths`. ALS v2 module bundles must no longer carry `.als/modules/<module>/v<N>/delamains/<delamain>/dispatcher/` — the compiler rejects that layout in `als_version >= 2` with reason `delamain.bundled_dispatcher.forbidden`, while v1 snapshots remain loadable as the upgrade source. `alsc deploy claude` now projects `.claude/delamains/<name>/dispatcher/` from the installed construct root rather than direct canonical overlay, and fails closed when the installed copy is missing for an `als_version >= 2` system. The construct-upgrade engine discovers and writes through `.als/constructs/delamain-dispatcher/<delamain>/`, no longer scanning module bundles. `construct.json.name`, prompt keys, lifecycle strategy ids, tests, and the `construct:dispatcher` target slug all stay on `dispatcher` — only the directory branding moves. SDR 043 (`Status: Accepted`) is the binding `v1 → v2` record. No dispatcher `VERSION` bump or sequential construct migration ships with this hop; the cutover is owned entirely by the new `language-upgrades/recipes/v1-to-v2/` bundle, which seeds installed constructs, archives the old bundled dispatcher trees as recipe-managed support backups under `.als/runtime/language-upgrades/v1-to-v2/dispatcher-bundle-backups/`, removes `delamains/<name>/dispatcher/` from authored module bundles, bumps `system.ts` `als_version` to `2`, and re-projects `.claude/`. The first frozen v2 fixture lands at `nfrith-repos/als/language-upgrades/fixtures/v2/`. Bundled skills `/new`, `/change`, and `/upgrade-delamain` and reference docs (`vocabulary.md`, `delamain-overview.md`, `delamain-dispatcher.md`, `module-integration.md`) are updated to teach the new construct boundary.
- Operator action: Required. Run `/update` — the update transaction detects the `als_version: 1 → 2` delta and executes the `v1-to-v2` recipe (single `rewrite-dispatcher-layout` step) which seeds `<system_root>/.als/constructs/delamain-dispatcher/<delamain>/` from the canonical, archives existing bundled dispatcher trees, deletes `delamains/<name>/dispatcher/` from authored bundles, bumps `als_version` to `2`, and re-projects `.claude/`. After the recipe lands, future dispatcher `VERSION` bumps ride the normal sequential construct-upgrade chain. Pre-`v11` migration tails are not restored — stale `v5/v8/v11` bundle copies are vendor drift, not migration inputs.
- Affected surfaces: `nfrith-repos/als/delamain-dispatcher/`, `nfrith-repos/als/language-upgrades/recipes/v1-to-v2/recipe.yaml`, `nfrith-repos/als/language-upgrades/recipes/v1-to-v2/scripts/apply-dispatcher-cutover.ts`, `nfrith-repos/als/language-upgrades/fixtures/v2/`, `nfrith-repos/als/alsc/compiler/src/contracts.ts`, `nfrith-repos/als/alsc/compiler/src/validate.ts`, `nfrith-repos/als/alsc/compiler/src/claude-skills.ts`, `nfrith-repos/als/alsc/upgrade-construct/src/engine.ts`, `nfrith-repos/als/alsc/upgrade-construct/src/known-fingerprints.ts`, `nfrith-repos/als/skills/new/SKILL.md`, `nfrith-repos/als/skills/change/SKILL.md`, `nfrith-repos/als/skills/upgrade-delamain/SKILL.md`, `nfrith-repos/als/skills/docs/references/vocabulary.md`, `nfrith-repos/als/skills/docs/references/delamain-overview.md`, `nfrith-repos/als/skills/docs/references/delamain-dispatcher.md`, `nfrith-repos/als/skills/docs/references/module-integration.md`, `nfrith-repos/als/sdr/043-delamain-dispatcher-source-location-contract.md`, `nfrith-repos/als/reference-system/.als/system.ts`, `nfrith-repos/als/reference-system/.als/constructs/delamain-dispatcher/`.

### ALS-076
- Compatibility: refresh_required, additive
- Summary: Add the cross-state concurrency pool primitive — a new optional top-level `concurrency_pools` map on the Delamain shape that declares "these N states share a single capacity of M" and gates dispatch across them. SDR 042 (`Status: Accepted`) at `nfrith-repos/als/sdr/042-delamain-cross-state-concurrency-pool-contract.md` is the canonical contract; ALS v1 stays valid (no `SUPPORTED_ALS_VERSIONS` widening, no `language-upgrades/recipes/v1-to-v2/`, matching the policy settled when ALS-073 was cancelled). The compiler addition in `nfrith-repos/als/alsc/compiler/src/delamain.ts` extends `delamainShapeSchema` with `concurrency_pools: z.record(delamainNameSchema, delamainConcurrencyPoolSchema).optional()` and adds graph-level `validateDelamainDefinition()` rules: pool members must exist on the same delamain, must be agent-owned non-terminal states, must form at least two distinct states, may not appear in two distinct pools; `capacity` is a positive integer; pooled states keep their per-state `concurrency` cap and both gates must have headroom before dispatch (preserving SDR 036's same-state semantics). The authored `concurrency_pools` field rides through the existing `loadedDelamain.shape` projection in `alsc/compiler/src/claude-skills.ts`, reaching deployed `delamain.yaml` without a second runtime-manifest channel. Dispatcher enforcement lands in the canonical `nfrith-repos/als/skills/new/references/dispatcher/` template: a new `src/concurrency.ts` centralizes `buildConcurrencySnapshot`, `evaluateDispatchConcurrency`, and `reserveDispatchConcurrency`; `src/dispatcher.ts` resolves authored pools into a `poolByState` lookup, stamps each agent-owned `DispatchEntry` with its pool, and exposes `concurrencyPools` on `ResolvedConfig`; `src/runtime-state.ts` adds `listPoolConcurrencyHolders`, `countPoolConcurrencyOccupancy`, and `evaluatePoolConcurrency` summing only `active`+`blocked` records across pool members (matching SDR 036's count rule — guarded and orphaned holders are deliberately excluded); `src/index.ts::tick()` reuses `prepareDispatch`'s output for the live `dispatch()` call (new `preparedInput` parameter on `dispatch`) and reserves pool capacity in-memory only after the runtime registry accepts the prepare, so a failed prepare cannot consume pool capacity and two queued items in different pooled states cannot oversubscribe one pool within a single tick. Suppression telemetry in `src/telemetry.ts` extends `dispatch_suppressed_concurrency` with a `blocked_by: "state" | "pool"` discriminator plus optional `pool_id`, `pool_states`, and normalized `pool_holders` (each holder names `{dispatch_id, item_id, state, status}`); when both a destination state's per-state cap and its pool cap exhaust on the same attempt, the event reports `blocked_by: "pool"` so the cross-state cause is explicit, and the same-state-only payload is unchanged when `blocked_by: "state"`. The dispatcher construct advances `nfrith-repos/als/skills/new/references/dispatcher/VERSION` 14 → 15 with `migrations/v14-to-v15.ts` documented as no-op (no persisted runtime-state shape change).
